┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ GO-TO-MARKET STRATEGY: THE FOUNDATION │ └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

CORE DEFINITION ════════════════

Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy \= The comprehensive plan for how you'll: ├─ Reach your target customers ├─ Convert them into paying users ├─ Deliver value efficiently └─ Scale revenue profitably

It's NOT just: ❌ Marketing strategy (that's one component) ❌ Sales strategy (that's one component) ❌ Product launch plan (that's one execution) ❌ Pricing model (that's one lever)

It IS: ✅ The complete orchestration of product, marketing, sales, and customer success to acquire and retain customers profitably

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

THE GTM STRATEGY STACK ════════════════════════

Think of GTM as layers that build on each other:

LAYER 1: MARKET STRATEGY (Foundation) ├─ Who: Target customer (ICP, personas) ├─ What: Product positioning (category, differentiation) ├─ Why: Value proposition (problem → solution → outcome) └─ Where: Market selection (segments, verticals, geos)

LAYER 2: REVENUE MODEL (Economics) ├─ Pricing: How much to charge ├─ Packaging: What's included in each tier ├─ Contract: Annual, multi-year, consumption └─ Unit economics: CAC, LTV, payback period

LAYER 3: ACQUISITION STRATEGY (How to Get Customers) ├─ Awareness: How they discover you ├─ Consideration: How they evaluate you ├─ Purchase: How they buy from you └─ Motion: PLG, sales-led, or hybrid

LAYER 4: CHANNEL STRATEGY (Where to Show Up) ├─ Direct: Your own sales/marketing ├─ Indirect: Partners, resellers, marketplaces ├─ Digital: Website, ads, content, SEO └─ Human: Inside sales, field sales, SDRs

LAYER 5: CUSTOMER SUCCESS (Retention & Expansion) ├─ Onboarding: Time to value ├─ Adoption: Usage, engagement ├─ Retention: Renewal rates └─ Expansion: Upsell, cross-sell

All layers must align. Misalignment \= GTM failure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHY GTM STRATEGY MATTERS ══════════════════════════

Bad GTM Strategy Costs: ├─ High CAC: $50K to acquire $30K ACV customer (underwater) ├─ Long sales cycles: 12+ months to close (burn cash) ├─ Low win rates: \<20% (wasted sales effort) ├─ Poor retention: 70% churn Year 1 (leaky bucket) ├─ Slow growth: Can't scale efficiently └─ Failed launches: Product doesn't gain traction

Good GTM Strategy Delivers: ├─ Efficient CAC: $15K to acquire $120K LTV customer (3-6mo payback) ├─ Fast sales cycles: 60-90 days (predictable revenue) ├─ High win rates: 40-60% (focused, effective) ├─ Strong retention: 95%+ NRR (compound growth) ├─ Scalable growth: 2-3x YoY └─ Successful launches: Products gain rapid adoption

GTM strategy determines: ├─ Can you acquire customers profitably? (Unit economics) ├─ Can you scale? (Repeatable motion) ├─ Can you defend against competitors? (Moat) └─ Will you survive? (Runway vs growth rate)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

GTM STRATEGY VS TACTICS ═════════════════════════

Common mistake: Confusing strategy with tactics

STRATEGY (The plan): ├─ Who we target: Mid-market IT teams (500-2,500 employees) ├─ How we position: Operational IGA (fast deployment, SaaS-first) ├─ How we sell: Hybrid (inside sales \+ product-led) ├─ What we charge: $4-8/user/month, transparent pricing └─ Why we win: 60-day deployment vs 18-month competitors

TACTICS (The execution): ├─ SEO campaign for "IGA software" keywords ├─ LinkedIn ads targeting IT Directors ├─ Free trial with guided onboarding ├─ SDR outbound to 1,000-2,500 employee SaaS companies └─ Case study content featuring 60-day deployments

Strategy \= WHY and WHAT Tactics \= HOW specifically

Strategy informs tactics. Not the other way around.

Bad: "Let's do more LinkedIn ads" (tactic-first) Good: "Our target (IT Directors) is on LinkedIn, so let's advertise there" (strategy-first)

THE GTM STRATEGY QUESTIONS ════════════════════════════

Every GTM strategy must answer 10 questions:

1. WHO is our ideal customer? (ICP specificity)  
2. WHY do they need us? (Problem acuteness)  
3. WHAT value do we deliver? (Outcome clarity)  
4. HOW do they discover us? (Awareness channels)  
5. WHERE do they evaluate? (Buyer journey)  
6. WHEN do they buy? (Purchase triggers)  
7. WHAT do we charge? (Pricing & packaging)  
8. HOW do they buy? (Sales process)  
9. WHO sells? (Sales org structure)  
10. HOW do we scale? (Repeatability)

Example (IRIS GTM Strategy):

1. WHO: Mid-market IT teams (500-2,500 employees, 100+ SaaS apps)  
2. WHY: Drowning in manual access management, need IGA but can't wait 18 months  
3. WHAT: Automate 85% of workflows, deploy in 60 days, audit-ready  
4. HOW: SEO (IGA searches), LinkedIn (IT Director targeting), referrals  
5. WHERE: Website, demos, references, free trial  
6. WHEN: Audit deadline, growth scaling, compliance requirement  
7. WHAT: $4-8/user/month, transparent, predictable  
8. HOW: Inside sales-led with product-qualified leads  
9. WHO: SDRs (qualify) → AEs (close) → CSMs (expand)  
10. HOW: Repeatable 60-day implementation, land & expand

All 10 questions answered \= Complete GTM strategy Missing answers \= Gaps to fill

GTM STRATEGY EVOLUTION BY STAGE ═════════════════════════════════

Your GTM strategy should evolve as you grow:

STAGE 1: Pre-PMF (0-$1M ARR) ───────────────────────────── Focus: Find product-market fit GTM: Founder-led sales, manual, anything that works ICP: Narrow (one segment, one persona) Sales: Founder sells, no process Marketing: Word-of-mouth, content Metric: Retention, usage, NPS

Strategy: ├─ Sell to anyone who will buy (learn) ├─ High-touch everything (doesn't scale, that's OK) ├─ Rapid iteration based on feedback └─ Narrow \> broad (focus beats breadth)

STAGE 2: Early Traction ($1M-5M ARR) ───────────────────────────────────── Focus: Prove repeatability GTM: Build initial sales process ICP: Still narrow (validate one segment first) Sales: First sales hires, basic playbook Marketing: Demand gen experiments, SEO, content Metric: Win rate, sales cycle, CAC payback

Strategy: ├─ Hire first AEs (prove founder can be replaced) ├─ Document what works (sales playbook v1) ├─ Test channels systematically (find 2-3 that work) └─ Don't expand too fast (nail one segment first)

STAGE 3: Scale ($5M-20M ARR) ──────────────────────────── Focus: Scale what works GTM: Systematic, multi-channel ICP: Can expand to 2-3 segments Sales: Team of AEs, SDRs, sales managers Marketing: Proven channels at scale, brand building Metric: Growth rate, efficiency (CAC ratio)

Strategy: ├─ Pour gas on working channels ├─ Hire ahead of demand (build capacity) ├─ Expand ICP carefully (adjacent segments) └─ Build systems & processes (playbooks, training)

STAGE 4: Efficiency ($20M-100M ARR) ──────────────────────────────────── Focus: Profitable scaling GTM: Optimized, specialized ICP: Multiple segments, verticals Sales: Specialized teams (SMB, MM, ENT) Marketing: Full-funnel, multi-channel, sophisticated Metric: Magic number, payback period, NRR

Strategy: ├─ Optimize unit economics (improve CAC, LTV) ├─ Specialize go-to-market (segment-specific) ├─ Build partner ecosystem (scale beyond direct) └─ International expansion (if applicable)

STAGE 5: Dominance ($100M+ ARR) ──────────────────────────────── Focus: Market leadership GTM: Multi-motion (enterprise, mid-market, PLG) ICP: Broad (serve entire market) Sales: Complex org, multiple sales motions Marketing: Brand-driven, category creation Metric: Market share, retention, expansion

Strategy: ├─ Own the category (be synonymous with solution) ├─ Multiple routes to market (serve all segments) ├─ Platform plays (ecosystem, integrations) └─ M\&A (buy vs build for new capabilities)

Common mistake: Using Stage 5 playbook at Stage 2 (Complex before you need to be)

Right approach: Use Stage-appropriate GTM (Simple → Complex as you grow)

COMPONENT 1: IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE (ICP) ═══════════════════════════════════════════

What it is: Detailed description of accounts that: ├─ Get the most value from your product ├─ Buy faster and easier ├─ Stay longest (high retention) ├─ Expand most (highest LTV) └─ Advocate most (referrals, case studies)

ICP Framework (4 Dimensions):

FIRMOGRAPHIC (Company traits): ├─ Employee count: \[Range\] ├─ Revenue: \[Range\] ├─ Industry: \[Verticals\] ├─ Geography: \[Regions\] └─ Growth stage: \[Startup / Scaleup / Enterprise\]

TECHNOGRAPHIC (Tech environment): ├─ Tech stack: \[Tools they use\] ├─ Cloud adoption: \[SaaS-first / Hybrid / On-prem\] ├─ IT maturity: \[Sophistication level\] ├─ Integration needs: \[What they connect to\] └─ Security posture: \[Compliance requirements\]

BEHAVIORAL (Buying patterns): ├─ Buying process: \[Committee / Individual\] ├─ Decision timeline: \[Fast / Slow\] ├─ Budget authority: \[Centralized / Distributed\] ├─ Vendor preferences: \[Best-of-breed / Suite\] └─ Risk tolerance: \[Early adopter / Conservative\]

PSYCHOGRAPHIC (Goals & pains): ├─ Primary goal: \[What they want to achieve\] ├─ Top pain: \[What frustrates them\] ├─ Success metric: \[How they measure success\] ├─ Career motivation: \[What drives decision-maker\] └─ Objections: \[What makes them hesitate\]

Example (IRIS ICP):

FIRMOGRAPHIC: ├─ Employees: 500-2,500 (mid-market sweet spot) ├─ Revenue: $50M-500M ├─ Industry: Tech, SaaS, FinTech (SaaS-heavy users) ├─ Geography: North America, Western Europe └─ Growth: Series B-D, scaling rapidly

TECHNOGRAPHIC: ├─ SaaS apps: 100-300 (need governance at scale) ├─ Cloud: 80%+ SaaS (not legacy on-prem) ├─ IT team: 5-20 people (need automation) ├─ Stack: Okta, Google Workspace, Salesforce, Slack (modern) └─ Compliance: Entering phase (SOC2, ISO, HIPAA)

BEHAVIORAL: ├─ Buying: IT \+ Security committee (2-4 people) ├─ Timeline: 2-4 months (faster than enterprise) ├─ Budget: $150K-400K IT/Security budget ├─ Preference: Best-of-breed \> suite (specialized tools) └─ Risk: Moderate (will try newer vendors if proven)

PSYCHOGRAPHIC: ├─ Goal: "Scale IT operations without scaling headcount" ├─ Pain: "Drowning in access request tickets" ├─ Metric: Hours saved, ticket reduction ├─ Motivation: Operational efficiency (not just compliance) └─ Objection: "Are you enterprise-ready?" (new vendor concern)

How to Use ICP: ├─ Sales: Focus ONLY on ICP accounts (higher win rate) ├─ Marketing: Target ICP characteristics in campaigns ├─ Product: Build features ICP needs (not everyone) ├─ CS: Optimize onboarding for ICP use cases └─ Pricing: Structure packages for ICP budget ranges

ICP Tiers: ├─ Tier 1 (Best fit): All characteristics match → 70%+ win rate ├─ Tier 2 (Good fit): Most match → 40-50% win rate ├─ Tier 3 (Risky): Few match → \<20% win rate (avoid)

Rule: Narrow ICP \= Higher win rate \+ faster sales cycles

COMPONENT 4: PRICING & PACKAGING ══════════════════════════════════

What it is: How you structure and charge for value

Key Pricing Decisions:

1. PRICING METRIC (Unit of value): ├─ Per user (SaaS standard) ├─ Per application (usage-based) ├─ Flat rate (simple but limits expansion) ├─ Consumption (usage-driven) └─ Value-based (% of ROI, outcomes)

IRIS: Per user/month ($4-8 based on tier, volume) Why: Aligns with how customers think (\# of employees)

1. PRICING TIERS (Good-better-best):

Typical SaaS Structure: ├─ Starter: Limited features, self-service (small companies) ├─ Professional: Core features, email support (mid-market) ├─ Enterprise: All features, dedicated support (large)

IRIS Example: ├─ Essentials: $4/user/mo (basic IGA, 5K users max) ├─ Professional: $6/user/mo (advanced features, 15K users max) ├─ Enterprise: $8/user/mo (unlimited, premium support)

1. PACKAGING (What's included):

Feature Gating: ├─ Tier 1: Core features only ├─ Tier 2: \+ Advanced reporting, integrations ├─ Tier 3: \+ Custom workflows, SSO, API access

Support Gating: ├─ Tier 1: Email support, community ├─ Tier 2: \+ Chat support, 24hr SLA ├─ Tier 3: \+ Phone, dedicated CSM, 4hr SLA

IRIS Packaging: ├─ All tiers: 60-day deployment, unlimited apps ├─ Differentiation: Features, support, scale └─ Philosophy: Don't limit value, limit features

1. CONTRACT TERMS: ├─ Monthly: Higher churn, easier to start ├─ Annual: Standard SaaS (12-month commitment) ├─ Multi-year: Discount for 2-3 year commitment └─ Usage: Pay as you grow (consumption-based)

IRIS: Annual contracts with monthly payment option Why: Predictable revenue, but flexible cash flow

1. DISCOUNTING STRATEGY: ├─ Volume: Discount for \>X users (incentivize larger deals) ├─ Multi-year: 10-15% off for 2-3 year commitment ├─ Competitive: Discount to win vs competitor (strategic) ├─ Startup: Special pricing for early-stage (land & expand)

IRIS Discounting: ├─ Standard: 10-20% for 500+ users ├─ Multi-year: 15% off for 3-year commitment ├─ Competitive: Case-by-case (beat competitor quote) └─ No: Deep discounts that harm economics

Pricing Psychology:

Anchoring: ├─ Show highest tier first (anchor high) ├─ Middle tier looks reasonable (90% choose this) └─ Lowest tier for frugal buyers

Transparency: ├─ Published pricing (IRIS does this) Pros: Qualifies leads, builds trust, faster sales Cons: Competitors see it, less negotiation room ├─ "Contact sales" pricing (Enterprise model) Pros: Flexibility, extract max value Cons: Friction, extends sales cycle

Pricing Testing: ├─ Test with 10-20 customers before broad launch ├─ Monitor: Win rate by tier, ACV distribution ├─ Adjust: Quarterly reviews, annual major changes └─ Avoid: Frequent changes (confuses market)

Pricing Mistakes: ❌ Too low: Leave money on table, commoditize ❌ Too high: Price out ICP, lose to competitors ❌ Too complex: Confuses buyers, slows sales ❌ Value-detached: Price not tied to value delivered ❌ Inflexible: Can't adapt to different segments

Good Pricing: ✅ Aligned with value: Pay more \= get more ✅ Simple: Easy to understand and explain ✅ Scalable: Grows with customer (expansion revenue) ✅ Competitive: Defensible vs alternatives ✅ Profitable: Supports healthy unit economics

COMPONENT 5: SALES PROCESS ════════════════════════════

What it is: Repeatable steps to convert leads to customers

Standard B2B SaaS Sales Process:

STAGE 1: QUALIFICATION (SDR-led) ───────────────────────────────── Goal: Determine if prospect fits ICP Activities: ├─ Discovery questions (BANT: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) ├─ Pain confirmation (Do they have the problem?) ├─ Fit assessment (Do they match ICP?) └─ Meeting scheduled (Pass to AE if qualified)

Qualification Criteria (IRIS): ├─ 500-2,500 employees (size check) ├─ 100+ SaaS apps (use case fit) ├─ IT or Security buyer (right persona) ├─ Budget: $150K-400K (economic fit) ├─ Timeline: \<6 months (urgency) └─ Trigger: Audit, growth, compliance (buying signal)

Pass/No-Pass: \>4 criteria met \= Pass to AE

STAGE 2: DISCOVERY (AE-led) ──────────────────────────── Goal: Understand problem deeply, build business case Activities: ├─ Problem exploration (pain, impact, cost) ├─ Current state (what they use today) ├─ Stakeholder mapping (who's involved in decision) ├─ Success criteria (what does "win" look like) └─ Demo scheduled (if pain confirmed)

Key Questions (IRIS): ├─ "How many access requests per week?" (quantify pain) ├─ "How long does onboarding take?" (baseline) ├─ "What's your audit timeline?" (urgency) ├─ "What have you tried before?" (alternatives, why failed) ├─ "Who else needs to approve?" (buying committee)

Output: Documented pain \+ business case

STAGE 3: DEMO/PRESENTATION (SE-led) ──────────────────────────────────── Goal: Show how product solves their specific problem Activities: ├─ Customized demo (their use case, their data if possible) ├─ Feature walkthrough (focus on relevant features only) ├─ ROI illustration (show their savings/value) ├─ Objection handling (address concerns live) └─ Next steps (trial, eval, or proposal)

Demo Best Practices: ├─ Customer-centric: Show THEIR problem → solution ├─ Specific: Use their company name, app names ├─ Outcome-focused: "Here's the result" \> "Here's how it works" ├─ Interactive: Let them drive, ask questions └─ Short: 30-45 minutes max (attention span)

IRIS Demo Flow:

1. Confirm pain (5 min): "You said 50 tickets/week..."  
2. Show automation (15 min): "Here's how IRIS automates..."  
3. Prove deployment (10 min): "Our 60-day implementation..."  
4. ROI calculation (5 min): "Based on your numbers, you'll save..."  
5. Q\&A (5 min): Handle objections

STAGE 4: EVALUATION (Mutual) ────────────────────────────── Goal: Let them validate fit with trial/POC/references Activities: ├─ Free trial (if PLG motion) ├─ POC/Pilot (for enterprise deals) ├─ Reference calls (similar customers) ├─ Technical validation (security review, integrations) └─ Business case refinement (finalize ROI)

IRIS Evaluation: ├─ Trial: 14-day full access (guided onboarding) ├─ References: 2-3 similar companies (size, use case) ├─ Security: Share SOC2, pen test results ├─ Timeline: 2-3 weeks (don't drag out)

STAGE 5: PROPOSAL (AE-led) ─────────────────────────── Goal: Present pricing, terms, business case Activities: ├─ Proposal document (pricing, scope, timeline) ├─ ROI calculator (their specific numbers) ├─ MSA/contract (legal terms) ├─ Stakeholder alignment (ensure all buyers on board) └─ Negotiation (terms, pricing, timeline)

IRIS Proposal Includes: ├─ Pricing: Tier recommendation \+ volume discount ├─ Scope: \# users, apps, implementation timeline ├─ ROI: Their savings calculation (hours, costs) ├─ References: Customer quotes, case studies ├─ Timeline: 60-day deployment guarantee └─ Terms: Annual contract, payment schedule

STAGE 6: CLOSE (AE-led) ──────────────────────── Goal: Get signature, kickoff implementation Activities: ├─ Contract negotiation (redlines, revisions) ├─ Legal review (mutual contract review) ├─ Signature (DocuSign, wet signature) ├─ Payment setup (invoicing, PO) └─ Implementation kickoff (handoff to CS)

Closing Tactics: ├─ Create urgency: "To hit your Q3 audit, we need to start by..." ├─ Remove friction: "I'll walk you through DocuSign now" ├─ Executive alignment: "Let's schedule brief call with my CEO and yours" ├─ Deadline: "This pricing expires end of quarter"

Sales Cycle Timeline (IRIS): ├─ Qualification: 1-2 weeks ├─ Discovery: 1-2 weeks ├─ Demo: 1 week ├─ Evaluation: 2-3 weeks ├─ Proposal: 1-2 weeks ├─ Close: 1-2 weeks └─ Total: 60-90 days (2-3 months)

Sales Process Metrics: ├─ Stage conversion rates (% moving to next stage) ├─ Stage duration (time in each stage) ├─ Win rate (closed-won / total opportunities) ├─ Sales cycle length (days from MQL to customer) └─ ACV by stage entry (predict revenue)

Example (IRIS Funnel): ├─ MQLs: 1,000/quarter ├─ SQLs (qualified): 300 (30% conversion) ├─ Opportunities: 150 (50% conversion) ├─ Closed-won: 45 (30% win rate) ├─ Average ACV: $180K └─ Revenue: $8.1M/quarter

WHAT IS A GTM MOTION? ═══════════════════════

GTM Motion \= The primary way customers discover, evaluate, and buy

Three Primary Motions: ├─ Product-Led Growth (PLG): Product drives acquisition ├─ Sales-Led Growth (SLG): Sales team drives acquisition └─ Hybrid/Combined: Mix of both

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

MOTION 1: PRODUCT-LED GROWTH (PLG) ════════════════════════════════════

Definition: Product itself is primary driver of acquisition, conversion, and expansion

How It Works: ├─ Free trial/freemium: Users can start without sales ├─ Self-serve: Sign up, onboard, use independently ├─ Virality: Product spreads through usage (invite teammates) ├─ Usage-based: Pay as you grow (low friction) └─ Expansion: Product drives upsell (hit limits → upgrade)

PLG Characteristics:

User Journey:

1. Discovery: Find via search, referral, content  
2. Trial: Sign up for free (credit card optional)  
3. Aha moment: Experience value quickly (minutes/hours)  
4. Adoption: Use regularly, see ongoing value  
5. Upgrade: Hit limits or want more features  
6. Expand: Invite team, increase usage

Sales Involvement: ├─ Early: None (fully self-serve) ├─ Later: Sales assists expansion (high-value accounts) └─ Model: Sales helps users who want to buy, not cold outreach

Product Requirements: ├─ Fast time-to-value: Must deliver value in \<1 hour ├─ Intuitive UX: No training required, obvious how to use ├─ Viral loops: Built-in sharing (invite team, collaborate) ├─ Freemium viable: Core value free, premium features paid └─ Self-serve onboarding: In-app guidance, no human needed

Examples (PLG Products): ├─ Slack: Free for small teams, viral (invite colleagues) ├─ Figma: Free for individuals, teams upgrade ├─ Notion: Freemium, spreads within organizations ├─ Dropbox: Free storage, pay for more └─ Calendly: Free basic, paid for features

When PLG Works: ✅ Low ACV ($1K-10K): Economics support self-serve ✅ Simple product: Easy to understand, quick setup ✅ Individual users: Can start without IT/procurement ✅ Bottom-up adoption: Users bring it in, then spread ✅ Network effects: More users \= more value

When PLG Doesn't Work: ❌ High ACV ($100K+): Needs sales team to justify investment ❌ Complex product: Requires implementation, training ❌ Enterprise sales: Procurement, security, compliance required ❌ Long time-to-value: Takes weeks/months to see results ❌ No viral element: Usage doesn't naturally spread

PLG Metrics: ├─ Sign-up rate: Visitors who start trial ├─ Activation rate: Users who experience aha moment ├─ Time to value: How long until first value ├─ PQL (Product Qualified Lead): Users showing buying intent ├─ Free-to-paid conversion: % who upgrade ├─ Viral coefficient: How many new users each user brings └─ Expansion revenue: Growth from existing users

PLG for B2B SaaS: ├─ Freemium: Free forever plan (limited features/users) ├─ Free trial: 14-30 days full access, then pay ├─ Reverse trial: Start paid, refund if not happy └─ Usage-based: Pay only for what you use

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

MOTION 2: SALES-LED GROWTH (SLG) ══════════════════════════════════

Definition: Sales team is primary driver of acquisition

How It Works: ├─ Outbound: SDRs/BDRs prospect target accounts ├─ Inbound: Marketing generates MQLs, sales qualifies ├─ Demos: AEs conduct live demos, discovery ├─ Proposals: Custom proposals, ROI calculations ├─ Negotiation: Sales negotiates terms, pricing └─ Close: Sales closes deal, hands to CS

SLG Characteristics:

User Journey:

1. Awareness: Marketing (SEO, ads, events) or outreach  
2. MQL: Download content, attend webinar, visit website  
3. SQL: SDR qualifies, passes to AE  
4. Discovery: AE explores pain, builds business case  
5. Demo: Solutions Engineer shows product  
6. Proposal: AE presents pricing, terms  
7. Close: Contract negotiation and signature

Sales Process: ├─ SDR/BDR: Prospect, qualify, book meetings ├─ AE (Account Executive): Run sales process, close ├─ SE (Solutions Engineer): Technical demos, POCs ├─ Sales Manager: Deal support, forecasting └─ Leadership: Executive alignment, strategic deals

Product Requirements: ├─ Demo-able: Can show value in 30-60 min demo ├─ Configurable: Can customize to customer needs ├─ ROI quantifiable: Can prove business value ├─ Integration-friendly: Connects to customer systems └─ Enterprise-ready: Security, compliance, SLAs

Examples (Sales-Led Products): ├─ Salesforce: Enterprise CRM, complex sales ├─ Workday: Enterprise HCM, long sales cycles ├─ SailPoint: Enterprise IGA, 6-18 month sales ├─ ServiceNow: Enterprise IT, large deals └─ Oracle: Enterprise databases, field sales

When SLG Works: ✅ High ACV ($50K+): Economics support sales team ✅ Complex product: Needs explanation, customization ✅ Long sales cycle: 3-12+ months evaluation ✅ Committee buying: Multiple stakeholders involved ✅ Competitive: Need sales to differentiate ✅ Implementation services: Not self-serve

When SLG Doesn't Work: ❌ Low ACV (\<$5K): Can't afford sales team ❌ Simple product: Doesn't need explanation ❌ Self-serve market: Buyers expect to try without sales ❌ Long time-to-revenue: Can't wait for sales cycles ❌ No differentiation: Sales can't add value

SLG Metrics: ├─ MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead): Engagement threshold ├─ SQL (Sales Qualified Lead): BANT qualified ├─ Opportunity: Active sales pursuit ├─ Pipeline: Total value of opportunities ├─ Win rate: % of opportunities won ├─ Sales cycle: Days from SQL to close ├─ ACV (Average Contract Value): Deal size └─ CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): Cost to acquire

SLG Team Structure (Example): ├─ SDRs: 1 per 2-3 AEs (inbound qualification) ├─ BDRs: 1 per 2-3 AEs (outbound prospecting) ├─ AEs: Quota: $1M-2M ARR/year ├─ SEs: 1 per 3-5 AEs (technical pre-sales) └─ Managers: 1 per 8-10 reps

MOTION 3: HYBRID (PLG \+ SLG Combined) ═══════════════════════════════════════

Definition: Product-led for entry, sales-led for expansion

How It Works: ├─ Bottom-up: Users start with free/trial (PLG) ├─ Product qualification: Product identifies expansion signals ├─ Sales engagement: Sales reaches out to high-value users ├─ Land & expand: Start small, grow large with sales help └─ Multi-tier: Self-serve for SMB, sales for enterprise

Hybrid Model Flavors:

A) PLG-First, Sales Assist: ├─ Entry: Free trial, self-serve (PLG) ├─ Conversion: Auto-upgrade to paid (PLG) ├─ Expansion: Sales engages when usage/spend reaches threshold └─ Example: Slack (free → teams → enterprise sales)

B) Sales-First, Product Accelerates: ├─ Entry: Sales-led acquisition (traditional) ├─ Onboarding: Product drives adoption ├─ Expansion: Product usage triggers upsells └─ Example: HubSpot (sales lands, product expands)

C) Segment-Based: ├─ SMB: Fully PLG (self-serve) ├─ Mid-market: Hybrid (trial \+ inside sales) ├─ Enterprise: Fully SLG (field sales) └─ Example: Dropbox (consumer PLG, business SLG)

Hybrid Best Practices:

Product Qualification (PQL): Track signals that indicate buying intent: ├─ Usage: Power users (daily active, key features) ├─ Team: Invited colleagues (viral adoption) ├─ Limits: Hitting free tier limits (need upgrade) ├─ Engagement: Exploring paid features (curiosity) ├─ Company: Works at target ICP company └─ Role: Has buying authority (title indicators)

PQL Scoring Example: ├─ \+10 points: Added 5+ team members ├─ \+10 points: Used product 10+ days in month ├─ \+15 points: Hit storage/feature limit ├─ \+20 points: Works at 500+ employee company ├─ \+20 points: Title includes "Director," "VP," "Head" └─ Threshold: 50+ points \= PQL, route to sales

Sales Rules of Engagement: ├─ Don't contact until PQL threshold (respect self-serve) ├─ Add value, don't interrupt (help, don't sell) ├─ Timing matters (contact when they hit limits/pain) ├─ Multi-channel: Email \+ in-app message \+ call └─ Consultative: "I noticed you're using X heavily. Here's how others in your situation..."

Hybrid Metrics: ├─ PLG metrics: Sign-ups, activation, free-to-paid ├─ SLG metrics: PQL→SQL conversion, win rate, ACV ├─ Combined: Blended CAC, LTV by motion └─ Optimization: % revenue from each motion

Example (IRIS Hybrid Motion):

Tier 1: SMB (Self-Serve PLG) ├─ \<500 employees ├─ Free 14-day trial ├─ Self-serve sign-up, onboarding ├─ No sales contact unless they request ├─ Upgrade via website (credit card) └─ ACV: $20K-50K

Tier 2: Mid-Market (Hybrid) ├─ 500-2,500 employees ├─ Free trial OR sales demo ├─ Product qualifies (PQL if high usage) ├─ Inside sales reaches out to assist ├─ Proposal \+ negotiation for larger deals └─ ACV: $100K-400K

Tier 3: Enterprise (Sales-Led) ├─ 2,500+ employees ├─ No free trial (security concerns) ├─ Field sales, full discovery ├─ Custom demos, POCs ├─ Complex negotiations, legal └─ ACV: $500K+

Why Hybrid? ├─ Efficiency: PLG low CAC for small deals ├─ Coverage: Sales high-touch for large deals ├─ Speed: PLG fast entry, sales accelerates expansion ├─ Flexibility: Buyers choose their path └─ Optimization: Match motion to deal size

MOTION SELECTION FRAMEWORK ════════════════════════════

How to Choose Your GTM Motion:

STEP 1: Assess Your Product ────────────────────────────

Questions: ├─ Can users start without sales? (Y \= PLG possible) ├─ Is setup \<1 hour? (Y \= PLG friendly) ├─ Is value obvious immediately? (Y \= PLG works) ├─ Can users self-onboard? (Y \= PLG viable) └─ Is it viral (invite others)? (Y \= PLG accelerator)

If all Yes → PLG If mostly Yes → Hybrid (PLG primary) If mostly No → Sales-Led

STEP 2: Assess Your Market ───────────────────────────

Questions: ├─ Is ACV \>$50K? (Y \= Sales-led likely) ├─ Are buyers executives? (Y \= Sales-led likely) ├─ Is sales cycle \>3 months? (Y \= Sales-led needed) ├─ Multiple stakeholders? (Y \= Sales helps) └─ Requires implementation? (Y \= Sales manages)

If all Yes → Sales-Led If mostly Yes → Hybrid (Sales primary) If mostly No → PLG

STEP 3: Consider Your Stage ────────────────────────────

Early Stage (\<$5M ARR): ├─ Focus: Find PMF, learn what works ├─ Motion: Whatever works (flexible) ├─ Team: Small, founders selling └─ Advice: Start simple (one motion)

Growth Stage ($5M-20M ARR): ├─ Focus: Scale what works ├─ Motion: Double down on working motion ├─ Team: Hire specialists └─ Advice: Don't add complexity too early

Scale Stage ($20M-100M ARR): ├─ Focus: Efficient growth ├─ Motion: Can layer in second motion (hybrid) ├─ Team: Multiple teams, specialization └─ Advice: Segment-specific motions

MOTION EVOLUTION ═════════════════

Don't lock in forever. Motions evolve:

Phase 1: Start Sales-Led ├─ Reason: Need to learn customer deeply ├─ Founder-led sales (high touch) ├─ Build product understanding └─ 0-100 customers

Phase 2: Add Self-Serve Elements ├─ Reason: Can't scale founder manually ├─ Add trial, demo videos ├─ Hire first AEs └─ 100-500 customers

Phase 3: Hybrid ├─ Reason: Efficient SMB, sales for enterprise ├─ Segment by deal size ├─ Different motions for different ICPs └─ 500-2,000 customers

Phase 4: Multi-Motion Excellence ├─ Reason: Serve entire market ├─ PLG for SMB, inside for mid-market, field for enterprise ├─ Specialized teams └─ 2,000+ customers

Common Mistake: Jumping to Phase 4 at Phase 1 (Trying to do everything before nailing anything)

Right Approach: Start simple → Add complexity as you scale

WHY GTM METRICS MATTER ═══════════════════════

Metrics tell you: ├─ Is GTM working? (Efficiency) ├─ Where are bottlenecks? (Optimization opportunities) ├─ Can we scale? (Unit economics) ├─ What to do next? (Prioritization) └─ Are we going to survive? (Runway)

Without metrics: ❌ Guessing what works (no data) ❌ Can't optimize (don't know what's broken) ❌ Can't scale (don't know what to pour gas on) ❌ Run out of money (don't see danger signs)

THE GTM METRICS HIERARCHY ═══════════════════════════

LEVEL 1: TOP-LINE METRICS (Executive Dashboard) ═════════════════════════════════════════════════

These are the metrics that determine company health:

1. ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) ────────────────────────────────── What: Total recurring revenue normalized to annual Why: Primary growth metric for SaaS Formula: (Monthly Recurring Revenue × 12\) or sum of annual contracts Target: 2-3x YoY growth (early stage), 40-60% (scale stage)

Example (IRIS): ├─ Month 1: $1M MRR \= $12M ARR ├─ Month 12: $2M MRR \= $24M ARR └─ Growth: 100% YoY

Components: ├─ New ARR: From new customers ├─ Expansion ARR: From upsells/cross-sells ├─ Contraction ARR: From downgrades ├─ Churn ARR: From lost customers └─ Net New ARR \= New \+ Expansion \- Contraction \- Churn

1. Net Revenue Retention (NRR) ─────────────────────────────── What: Revenue retention \+ expansion from existing customers Why: Shows product value, expansion capability Formula: (Starting ARR \+ Expansion \- Contraction \- Churn) / Starting ARR Target: \>100% (expansion \> churn), best-in-class \>120%

Example (IRIS): ├─ Jan 1 ARR (cohort): $10M ├─ Expansion: \+$1.5M (upsells, more users) ├─ Contraction: \-$0.2M (downgrades) ├─ Churn: \-$0.5M (lost customers) ├─ Dec 31 ARR: $10.8M └─ NRR: $10.8M / $10M \= 108%

What it means: ├─ \>100%: Growing from existing customers (good) ├─ 90-100%: Slight churn, need improvement ├─ \<90%: Serious churn problem (fix this)

1. CAC Payback Period ────────────────────── What: Months to recover customer acquisition cost Why: Shows efficiency of customer acquisition Formula: CAC / (ACV × Gross Margin) Target: \<12 months (ideal \<6 months)

Example (IRIS): ├─ CAC: $18K ├─ ACV: $180K ├─ Gross Margin: 80% ├─ Annual Gross Profit: $144K ├─ Monthly Gross Profit: $12K └─ Payback: $18K / $12K \= 1.5 months ✅

Fast payback \= Can scale aggressively

1. LTV:CAC Ratio ───────────────── What: Lifetime value vs cost to acquire Why: Shows long-term profitability of customer acquisition Formula: LTV / CAC Target: \>3:1 (ideal 4:1 to 5:1)

Example (IRIS): ├─ LTV: $540K (calculated below) ├─ CAC: $18K └─ Ratio: $540K / $18K \= 30:1 ✅ (excellent)

What it means: ├─ \>5:1: Under-investing in growth (leave money on table) ├─ 3-5:1: Healthy, sustainable ├─ \<3:1: Over-spending on acquisition (not profitable)

1. Magic Number (Sales Efficiency) ─────────────────────────────────── What: ARR growth per dollar of sales/marketing spend Why: Shows efficiency of go-to-market investment Formula: (New ARR in Quarter × 4\) / (Sales & Marketing Spend Previous Quarter) Target: \>0.75 (efficient), \>1.0 (very efficient)

Example (IRIS Q2): ├─ Q1 Sales & Marketing Spend: $1.5M ├─ Q2 New ARR: $600K ├─ Annualized New ARR: $2.4M └─ Magic Number: $2.4M / $1.5M \= 1.6 ✅

What it means: ├─ \>1.0: Very efficient, scale aggressively ├─ 0.75-1.0: Efficient, continue investing ├─ \<0.75: Inefficient, fix before scaling

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

LEVEL 2: FUNNEL METRICS (Operational Dashboard) ═════════════════════════════════════════════════

These metrics show where you're strong/weak in the funnel:

AWARENESS STAGE: ──────────────── ├─ Website Traffic: Unique visitors/month ├─ Branded Search Volume: Searches for your brand ├─ Media Impressions: Reach of PR, content ├─ Social Followers: LinkedIn, Twitter following └─ Share of Voice: % of category conversations

CONSIDERATION STAGE: ──────────────────── ├─ MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead): Engaged prospects ├─ MQL Rate: MQLs / Website Visitors (conversion %) ├─ MQL→SQL Conversion: % of MQLs that become SQLs ├─ Cost per MQL: Total marketing spend / MQLs └─ Lead Velocity: Rate of MQL growth (MoM)

EVALUATION STAGE: ───────────────── ├─ SQL (Sales Qualified Lead): Qualified by sales ├─ Opportunity: Active sales pursuit ├─ Pipeline: Total value of opportunities ├─ Pipeline Coverage: Pipeline / Quota (should be 3-5x) └─ Pipeline Velocity: Speed of pipeline growth

DECISION STAGE: ─────────────── ├─ Win Rate: % of opportunities won ├─ Sales Cycle Length: Days from SQL to close ├─ ACV (Average Contract Value): Avg deal size ├─ Close Rate by Stage: Conversion at each stage └─ Competitor Win Rate: Win rate vs each competitor

RETENTION STAGE: ──────────────── ├─ Logo Retention: % customers who renew ├─ ARR Retention: % ARR retained (accounts for expansion) ├─ GRR (Gross Revenue Retention): Retention before expansion ├─ NRR (Net Revenue Retention): Retention \+ expansion └─ Churn Rate: % customers or ARR lost

LEVEL 3: UNIT ECONOMICS (Financial Dashboard) ═══════════════════════════════════════════════

These metrics determine if you can build a profitable business:

1. CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) ─────────────────────────────────── What: Fully loaded cost to acquire one customer Why: Must be recoverable within reasonable time Formula: (Sales \+ Marketing Spend) / New Customers Target: \<25% of ACV (for SaaS)

Example (IRIS): ├─ Sales Spend: $2M/quarter (salaries, commissions, tools) ├─ Marketing Spend: $1M/quarter (ads, content, events) ├─ Total: $3M ├─ New Customers: 54 └─ CAC: $3M / 54 \= $55,555

Components: ├─ Fully loaded: Salaries, benefits, tools, overhead ├─ Sales: AE, SDR, SE, sales ops, leadership ├─ Marketing: Demand gen, content, ads, events, tools └─ Period: Use same period as customers acquired

1. LTV (Lifetime Value) ──────────────────────── What: Total gross profit from customer over lifetime Why: Must be \>3x CAC to be profitable Formula: (ACV × Gross Margin) / Churn Rate Target: \>$500K for enterprise SaaS

Example (IRIS): ├─ ACV: $180K ├─ Gross Margin: 80% ├─ Annual Gross Profit: $144K ├─ Churn Rate: 5% annually (95% retention) └─ LTV: $144K / 0.05 \= $2.88M

Simplified LTV (if multi-year): ├─ ACV: $180K ├─ Avg customer life: 5 years ├─ Gross Margin: 80% └─ LTV: $180K × 5 × 80% \= $720K

1. Rule of 40 ────────────── What: Growth rate \+ Profit margin Why: Balances growth and profitability Formula: Revenue Growth Rate (%) \+ EBITDA Margin (%) Target: \>40%

Example (IRIS): ├─ Revenue Growth: 80% YoY ├─ EBITDA Margin: \-15% (burning for growth) └─ Rule of 40: 80% \- 15% \= 65% ✅

What it means: ├─ \>40%: Healthy (can grow or be profitable) ├─ \<40%: Struggling (slow growth AND unprofitable)

Stage-Appropriate Expectations: ├─ Early growth: High growth, negative margin (e.g. 100% \- 50% \= 50%) ├─ Scale: Moderate growth, breakeven (e.g. 50% \+ 0% \= 50%) ├─ Mature: Slower growth, profitable (e.g. 20% \+ 25% \= 45%)

1. Burn Multiple ───────────────── What: How much you burn per dollar of ARR growth Why: Capital efficiency metric Formula: Net Burn / Net New ARR Target: \<1.5 (efficient), \<1.0 (very efficient)

Example (IRIS): ├─ Quarterly Net Burn: $3M ├─ Quarterly Net New ARR: $2.4M └─ Burn Multiple: $3M / $2.4M \= 1.25

What it means: ├─ \<1.0: Very efficient (burning less than ARR growth) ├─ 1.0-1.5: Efficient ├─ \>1.5: Inefficient (burning too much per ARR added)

1. Months of Runway ──────────────────── What: How long until you run out of money Why: Survival metric Formula: Cash Balance / Monthly Burn Target: \>18 months (comfortable), \>12 months minimum

Example (IRIS): ├─ Cash: $24M ├─ Monthly Burn: $1M └─ Runway: 24 months ✅

When to raise: ├─ 12-18 months runway: Start fundraising process ├─ \<12 months: Urgently need capital or cut burn ├─ \>24 months: Comfortable, can be selective

METRICS MISTAKES TO AVOID ═══════════════════════════

❌ Vanity Metrics Mistake: Tracking metrics that look good but don't matter Examples: ├─ Website traffic (without conversion) ├─ Social followers (without engagement) ├─ Email list size (without opens/clicks) └─ Demo requests (without close rate)

Fix: Track only metrics that drive revenue or learning

❌ Too Many Metrics Mistake: Tracking 50+ metrics, can't focus Result: Analysis paralysis, no action Fix: 5-10 key metrics per dashboard level

❌ No Benchmarks Mistake: Don't know if metrics are good or bad Result: Can't assess performance Fix: Set targets based on stage/industry benchmarks

❌ Lagging Only Mistake: Only track outcome metrics (ARR, revenue) Result: Can't predict or influence future Fix: Balance lagging (outcomes) \+ leading (inputs)

❌ No Action Mistake: Track metrics but don't act on insights Result: Waste of time tracking Fix: Every metric review \= action items with owners

❌ Wrong Cohorts Mistake: Mix all customers together in analysis Result: Miss segment-specific insights Fix: Analyze by cohort (sign-up month, segment, channel)

❌ Ignoring Trends Mistake: Look at point-in-time only Result: Miss directional changes Fix: Always show trend (MoM, QoQ, YoY)

❌ No Context Mistake: Show metrics without comparison Result: Can't tell if good or bad Fix: Always show: Target, Previous Period, YoY

Good Metric Reporting: ├─ Actual vs Target: Am I on track? ├─ Trend: Am I improving or declining? ├─ Context: How does this compare to benchmark? ├─ Why: What's driving this number? └─ So What: What should we do about it?

┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ 10 DEADLY GTM STRATEGY MISTAKES │ └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

❌ MISTAKE \#1: NO CLEAR ICP ────────────────────────────

Symptom: "We sell to everyone" or ICP is too broad

Example: "Our ICP is B2B companies who need software" (That's literally everyone)

Why it fails: ├─ Messaging generic (can't resonate with anyone) ├─ Sales unfocused (chase wrong accounts) ├─ Marketing inefficient (spray and pray) ├─ Product confused (try to serve everyone) └─ Win rate low (no specialization advantage)

How to fix: ├─ Pick ONE segment to start (narrow \> broad) ├─ Be specific: Size, industry, tech stack, pain ├─ Test with 20-30 customers (do they match?) ├─ Iterate based on who you win/lose └─ Expand ICP only after nailing first segment

Good ICP (IRIS): "Mid-market IT teams (500-2,500 employees) at SaaS-first companies managing 100+ applications, entering compliance phase, with $150K-400K IT/Security budget" → Specific, actionable, focused

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

❌ MISTAKE \#2: WRONG GTM MOTION FOR PRODUCT ─────────────────────────────────────────────

Symptom: Motion doesn't match product/market

Example: "We have complex enterprise product with 6-month implementation, but we're doing PLG with free trial" → Users sign up, get confused, churn

Why it fails: ├─ Product requires explanation (can't self-serve) ├─ Long time to value (trial too short) ├─ High friction (complex setup) ├─ No support (users get stuck) └─ Bad experience (hurts brand)

How to fix: ├─ Match motion to product complexity ├─ Simple product → PLG works ├─ Complex product → Sales-led needed ├─ High ACV → Sales-led (economics support it) └─ Start with motion that fits, evolve later

Right Motion (IRIS): ├─ Product: Moderate complexity ├─ ACV: $180K average ├─ Motion: Hybrid (trial available \+ inside sales assist) └─ Result: Efficient mid-market coverage

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

❌ MISTAKE \#3: PREMATURE SCALING ──────────────────────────────────

Symptom: Scale before proving repeatable

Example: "We have 5 customers, let's hire 20 sales reps\!" → Reps don't know how to sell, burn cash

Why it fails: ├─ No proven playbook (reps don't know what works) ├─ Founder can't be replicated (unique relationships/approach) ├─ High burn (payroll before revenue) ├─ Chaos (too many moving parts) └─ Failure compounds (more people \= more problems)

How to fix: ├─ Prove repeatability first (3-5 reps hitting quota) ├─ Document playbook (what works, what doesn't) ├─ Hire gradually (don't 2x team overnight) ├─ Maintain tight feedback loops └─ Scale only when Magic Number \>0.75

Scaling Timeline: ├─ 0-$1M ARR: Founder-led, no scaling yet ├─ $1M-5M: Add 2-3 reps, prove repeatability ├─ $5M-20M: Scale team (but carefully) └─ $20M+: Aggressive scaling (proven motion)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

❌ MISTAKE \#4: IGNORING UNIT ECONOMICS ────────────────────────────────────────

Symptom: Don't know CAC, LTV, payback period

Example: "We're growing 200% YoY\!" Reality: Spending $100K to acquire $30K ACV customers → Growing into bankruptcy

Why it fails: ├─ Unsustainable (losing money on every customer) ├─ Can't raise capital (investors see the math) ├─ Run out of runway (burn too fast) ├─ Forced to cut (layoffs, panic) └─ Company fails despite "growth"

How to fix: ├─ Calculate CAC, LTV immediately (even rough) ├─ Target LTV:CAC \>3:1 minimum ├─ Payback \<12 months (ideally \<6) ├─ Track monthly (catch problems early) └─ Fix economics before scaling

Unit Economics (Required): ├─ CAC: Fully loaded cost per customer ├─ LTV: Lifetime gross profit per customer ├─ Payback: Months to recover CAC ├─ LTV:CAC: Must be \>3:1 └─ Track by channel, segment, cohort

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

❌ MISTAKE \#5: NO SALES/MARKETING ALIGNMENT ─────────────────────────────────────────────

Symptom: Sales and marketing point fingers

Example: Marketing: "We send you leads, you don't convert" Sales: "Your leads are junk, not qualified" → Blame game, no progress

Why it fails: ├─ MQLs not qualified (marketing volume game) ├─ Sales doesn't follow up (don't trust quality) ├─ No feedback loop (what works, what doesn't) ├─ Metrics misaligned (marketing \= MQLs, sales \= revenue) └─ Infighting (wasted energy)

How to fix: ├─ Define MQL together (agreed criteria) ├─ SLA: Marketing delivers X MQLs, Sales follows up in Y hours ├─ Closed-loop feedback: Sales tells marketing what converts ├─ Shared metrics: Pipeline, revenue (not just MQLs) └─ Weekly sync: Review funnel, adjust together

Alignment Framework: ├─ Shared ICP: Both target same accounts ├─ Shared metrics: Both measured on pipeline/revenue ├─ Agreed handoff: Clear MQL definition, SLA ├─ Feedback loop: Sales tells marketing what works └─ Collaboration: Joint account planning, campaigns

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

❌ MISTAKE \#6: FEATURE PARITY WITH EVERYONE ─────────────────────────────────────────────

Symptom: Try to match every competitor feature

Example: "Competitor X has feature Y, we MUST build it" → Roadmap driven by competitors, not customers → Product bloat, no differentiation

Why it fails: ├─ Never catch up (always behind) ├─ Lose differentiation (become me-too) ├─ Product bloat (complexity, confusion) ├─ No innovation (reactive, not proactive) └─ Customers don't care (many features go unused)

How to fix: ├─ Build from customer need (not competitor) ├─ Differentiate on dimension you own (don't copy) ├─ Accept gaps (you won't have every feature) ├─ Focus on unique strengths (double down) └─ Let competitor do their thing (you do yours)

IRIS Strategy: ├─ Don't match: SailPoint's on-premise depth ├─ Do differentiate: 60-day deployment vs 18 months ├─ Accept: We won't win every deal (that's OK) └─ Focus: SaaS-first, operational IGA, speed

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

❌ MISTAKE \#7: OVER-RELIANCE ON ONE CHANNEL ─────────────────────────────────────────────

Symptom: 80%+ of pipeline from one channel

Example: "LinkedIn ads are working great\! 90% of our leads" → LinkedIn changes algorithm or raises prices → Pipeline dries up overnight

Why it fails: ├─ Single point of failure (channel changes \= disaster) ├─ Channel saturation (audience fatigues) ├─ Price increases (lose negotiating power) ├─ Competitive pressure (others pile in) └─ No diversification (risky)

How to fix: ├─ Diversify: Build 3-5 working channels ├─ No channel \>40% of pipeline (spread risk) ├─ Test constantly (always have backups) ├─ Owned \> paid (SEO, community less risky than ads) └─ Monitor health (track effectiveness by channel)

Channel Mix (Healthy): ├─ SEO/Content: 30% (owned) ├─ LinkedIn Ads: 25% (paid) ├─ Referrals: 20% (earned) ├─ G2/Reviews: 15% (earned) ├─ Events: 10% (mixed) └─ No single channel \>40%

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

❌ MISTAKE \#8: PRICING TOO LOW ───────────────────────────────

Symptom: Race to bottom on price

Example: "Let's undercut competitors by 50% to win deals" → Attract price-sensitive customers → Can't afford to deliver value → High churn

Why it fails: ├─ Wrong customers (price-shoppers, not value-seekers) ├─ Can't invest (no margin for support, product) ├─ Commoditized (price is only differentiation) ├─ Hard to raise (customer expectations set) └─ Death spiral (cut price → cut costs → worse product → churn)

How to fix: ├─ Price on value (not cost or competition) ├─ Test willingness to pay (talk to customers) ├─ Premium OK (if you deliver value) ├─ Raise prices gradually (inflation, value adds) └─ Fire bad customers (price-only buyers who churn)

Pricing Philosophy (IRIS): ├─ Mid-market premium (not cheapest, not most expensive) ├─ $4-8/user/month (vs $3 low-end, $15 enterprise) ├─ Value justification: 60-day deployment, 85% automation ├─ Not competing on price (competing on speed \+ value) └─ Willing to lose price-sensitive deals

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

❌ MISTAKE \#9: NO ONBOARDING/CS INVESTMENT ────────────────────────────────────────────

Symptom: Focus on sales, ignore post-sale

Example: "We closed 50 customers but 30 churned Year 1" → Acquire, don't retain (leaky bucket)

Why it fails: ├─ Customer doesn't see value (bad onboarding) ├─ Churn negates growth (new ARR lost to churn) ├─ Bad reputation (unhappy customers \= bad reviews) ├─ Expensive (cheaper to retain than acquire) └─ No expansion (can't upsell churned customers)

How to fix: ├─ Invest in CS early (at $1M ARR, not $10M) ├─ Structured onboarding (repeatable, fast time-to-value) ├─ Proactive CS (monitor usage, reach out) ├─ NRR target \>100% (expansion \> churn) └─ Onboarding \= part of sale (not afterthought)

CS Investment (IRIS): ├─ Hire first CSM at $2M ARR (1:50 customer ratio) ├─ 60-day onboarding guarantee (contractual commitment) ├─ Proactive outreach (weekly check-ins first 90 days) ├─ QBRs: Quarterly value reporting └─ Result: 95% GRR, 112% NRR

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

❌ MISTAKE \#10: STRATEGY-DU-JOUR SYNDROME ───────────────────────────────────────────

Symptom: Change GTM strategy every quarter

Example: Q1: "We're doing PLG\!" Q2: "Actually, let's do enterprise sales" Q3: "Wait, let's try channel partners" Q4: "Back to PLG?" → No strategy sticks, nothing works

Why it fails: ├─ No time to learn (pivot before data) ├─ Team whiplash (constant changes, no execution) ├─ No optimization (can't improve what you abandon) ├─ Lost credibility (team stops believing) └─ Failure self-fulfilling (nothing gets chance to work)

How to fix: ├─ Commit to strategy for 6-12 months minimum ├─ Give it time (most things take 6 months to work) ├─ Iterate, don't pivot (optimize, don't abandon) ├─ Data-driven changes (not gut feeling) └─ Strategy endurance (see it through)

Strategy Commitment: ├─ Choose thoughtfully (research, validate) ├─ Commit fully (6-12 months minimum) ├─ Measure rigorously (track what works) ├─ Optimize continuously (improve, don't abandon) └─ Pivot only if clear failure (data-driven)

Example (IRIS): ├─ Year 1: Committed to hybrid motion ├─ Year 2: Doubled down (optimized, didn't change) ├─ Result: Motion working, predictable growth └─ Not switching until data says to
